What We Do




The global effort to stop the spread of COVID-19 has led to society losing touch. Losing
touch with everyday routines, losing touch with one another, and losing touch in its literal
sense. COVID-19 has created a new consciousness that high-touch surfaces are contaminated
with germs and other viruses that can make people ill. With this, the public has become
increasingly fearful of touching common surfaces resulting in “touch stigma”. Touch is
necessary given the design of society, but individuals with sight loss rely on touch as
a primary means to navigate the environment and are further negatively impacted.
Here is our solution.
