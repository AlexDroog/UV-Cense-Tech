FAQ


What is touch stigma?


It is a collection of negative attitudes or discrimination against the act of touching
and its conductivity to spreading COVID-19. Knowing this, have you or someone you know
been stigmatized for touching surfaces during the pandemic?



Is the UV light dangerous?


The UV - C light is the main component of UV - CENSE. This UV-C light in our products
is enclosed and has no contact with the eyes or skin. The handheld device has little to
no contact with the eyes or skin. The UV-Cense products are safe to use if used properly.
Visit the report on UV-Cense for more information.



What products are created?


UV-C light enclosed
UV-C light wand
UV-C light on tracks



How long does it take to sanitize?


This disinfection process would take approximately 20 seconds depending on the surface
area to disinfect.



Who Is This Product For?


This product is a solution to the current touch stigma. Thus, aiding those in the sight
loss community. However, this product helps promote accessibility to all.



What is the certification program?


A training program in partnership with the CNIB that educates in several disciplines and
ensures effective use of the products. After the certification has been completed, the user
can advertise to the public with a provided seal of approval.



What other viruses does UV-Cense Kill?


UV-Cense kills germs beyond COVID-19 such as all SARS family viruses, tuberculous and
common flu viruses



How to maintain


To maintain the systems, the UV-C light bulb will need to produce about 20,000 joules
per metre squared to be effective (Brend, 2020). UV-C light bulbs need to be replaced
at approximately 10,000 hours or as a rule of thumb should be replaced every year (Brend, 2020).
The passive infrared sensor (PIR) sensor that would be integrated into the sphere model
does not generally wear out so should not be expected to be subject to regular maintenance (Mathas, 2021).
