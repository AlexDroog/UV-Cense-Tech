Concept


The Design


The product is designed as an enclosed unit that automatically scans a surface after
the sensor indicates it has been touched. This disinfection process would approximately
take 20 seconds to achieve the elimination of 99.979% of COVID-19 pathogens and 10-30
seconds depending on the surface area to disinfect for other pathogenic microorganisms.
(CleanSlate, 2020, para 3; Seladi-Schulman, 2020)



Initially there will be three different UV-Cense systems designed. The primary system
will contain a UV-C light that is enclosed by a plastic shell. This system will be
utilized to sanitize small objects that can be entirely enclosed (ie: pin pads).



The second system is composed of a UV-C wand that is an object facing and runs on tracks
to sanitize large/flat surfaces.



The third system will be a UV-C wand that is used by trained sighted employees and sight
loss individuals to sanitize walls and table corners. As explained by Dr Sukhai, Head of
Research and Chief Accessibility Officer for the CNIB, table corners and walls are
frequentlyused as guides/markers for sight loss individuals when navigating spaces and
as such cannot be overlooked.
