About


Our Mission Statement


UV-Cense values equity, inclusion, and the betterment of society. Our mission
is to raise awareness for the impact of touch stigma. UV-Cense aims to meet the
three pillars of the triple bottom line, people, planet, and profit.



Bring Awareness


As emphasized by Dr. Sukhai, although the current health and safety guidelines
stigmatize the navigational practices of those with sight loss, this exclusion
stems from deeper oversight regarding the conflicts those with sight loss encounter
daily. Meaning that, the lack of awareness around the challenges experienced by
those with sight loss is not a new phenomenon; but rather, an existing issue
complicated by the impact of COVID-19 which highlighted the need for integral
change. In recognition of this oversight, UV-Cense has established a contact
with the CNIB, Dr Sukhai, who has helped to distribute surveys on the topic of
touch stigma to gain the insight of those with sight loss. There are high hopes
that a partnership with the CNIB will serve as a means to create change in the way
society combats the transmission of viruses, bacteria, and germs on all high touch
surfaces. In addressing the shortcomings in guidelines which support the sight loss
community, UV-Cense hopes to provide the CNIB with a forum to advocate for equity and
inclusion while affecting positive change.
